Working student positions are one of the most important pathways into the professional hunter jumper world — providing aspiring equestrians with hands-on experience, riding opportunities, and professional mentorship in exchange for barn work and horse care assistance. Elite Equine Marketplace lists working student positions from hunter jumper barns across the USA, connecting motivated young riders with the right programs for their goals and stage of development.

What Is a Working Student Position?

A working student position is an arrangement where a rider provides labor — typically daily horse care, grooming, tacking, barn cleaning, and show preparation — in exchange for some combination of riding instruction, riding time on program horses, housing, and professional mentorship. The specific terms of working student arrangements vary widely between programs — some offer extensive riding and coaching, others focus more on horse care skills and exposure to professional competition. Before accepting a working student position, always clarify exactly what is included in terms of riding time, instruction, housing, and compensation.

Florida Circuit Working Student Jobs — WEF & Ocala

The Florida winter circuit is one of the most sought-after destinations for working students — an opportunity to spend several weeks or months learning from top professionals at the world's best hunter jumper competition. WEF Wellington and WEC/HITS Ocala circuit working student positions are in high demand and fill quickly. Applicants for Florida circuit positions are advised to begin their search in the fall preceding the January circuit start.
